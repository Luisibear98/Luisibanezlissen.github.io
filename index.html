01 01 / Introduction

About Me

As a computer scientist and cybersecurity researcher, my work sits at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence Safety, Security, and Alignment. My PhD thesis at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid was recognized with the national award for the Best Cybersecurity Thesis in Spain, highlighting my research on fake news propagation, LLM deception, and adversarial robustness.

I collaborate globally with institutions such as Cambridge University, INRIA Paris, and FAR.AI. My research focuses on eliciting and analyzing steganographic behaviors in Large Language Models (LLMs), evaluating jailbreak vulnerability vectors on frontier models, and designing robust, privacy-preserving continuous authentication frameworks.

Beyond academia, I lead the Responsible AI team at Banco Santander, driving the bank's transition toward safe, fair, and transparent AI workflows. I am also an active speaker (sharing insights at venues like T3chFest), a Google TensorFlow Certified Developer, and an avid longboarding product developer.

04 04 / Publications & Preprints

Research Publications

Peer-Reviewed Publications

NeurIPS 2025 Core A*

Large language models can learn and generalize steganographic chain-of-thought under process supervision

L. Ibáñez-Lissen, et al.

Key Contribution: Demonstrates that RL-based process supervision can inadvertently incentivize LLMs to hide reasoning (steganography) inside Chain-of-Thought paths, bypassing standard alignment oversight. Done in collaboration with Cambridge University.

ESORICS 2025 Core A

LUMIA: Linear probing for Unimodal and MultiModal Membership Inference Attacks leveraging internal LLM states

L. Ibáñez-Lissen, et al.

Key Contribution: Applies linear probes to internal representations of text/image models to detect with high accuracy whether a specific sample was present in an LLM or LMM training corpus.

Journal of Information Security and Applications (JISA) Journal Q1

LPASS: Linear Probes as Stepping Stones for vulnerability detection using compressed LLMs

L. Ibáñez-Lissen, et al.

Key Contribution: Implements linear probing as an interpretable diagnostic tool to find optimal layers to prune or compress in LLMs without degrading vulnerability-detection accuracy.

ARES 2024 Core B

Continuous Authentication Leveraging Matrix Profile

L. Ibáñez-Lissen, et al.

Key Contribution: Introduces an incremental Matrix Profile framework to perform real-time continuous user authentication in a privacy-preserving manner, hitting 99% accuracy on streaming telemetry data.

IEEE Transactions on Big Data Journal Q1

Use of transfer learning for affordable in-context fake review generation

L. Ibáñez-Lissen, et al.

Key Contribution: Details how fine-tuned and transfer-learned smaller LLMs can generate hyper-realistic, context-aware fake reviews at massive scale, effectively bypassing SOTA industrial classification detectors.

Information Fusion / Information Systems Frontiers Journal Q1

Poisoning multitask multi-modal foundational generalistic models

L. Ibáñez-Lissen, et al.

Key Contribution: Investigates the threat landscape of backdoor data-poisoning attacks on multimodal foundational models (LMMs), charting how backdoors transfer fluidly across cross-modal domains and tasks.

IEEE Transactions on Computational Social Systems Journal Q1

On the Feasibility of Predicting Volumes of Fake News—The Spanish Case

L. Ibáñez-Lissen, et al.

Key Contribution: Models historical Spanish misinformation volume using multi-modal deep networks, demonstrating that large-scale fake news waves can be predicted with an average error of only 10%.
